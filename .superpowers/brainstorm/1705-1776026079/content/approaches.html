3 Architecture Approaches

How should InternshipHunter discover companies and find contacts?

A

Full AI Agent

One autonomous agent browses the web freely — searches Google, scrapes job boards, finds LinkedIn profiles, discovers emails, and drafts messages. You give it a location + field and it does everything.

Pros

  • Most flexible & powerful
  • Can find obscure companies
  • Adapts intelligently

Cons

  • Slowest (agent loops)
  • Most expensive (LLM calls)
  • Harder to control/debug
B

Structured Pipeline

Fixed steps: job board APIs (LinkedIn/Indeed) → contact APIs (Hunter.io/Apollo) → AI drafts. Each step is deterministic. Fast and cheap.

Pros

  • Fast & predictable
  • Cheap per run
  • Easy to debug

Cons

  • Limited to what APIs surface
  • Misses cold outreach targets
  • API costs + rate limits
C

Hybrid ⭐ Recommended

Job board APIs for active hiring companies + AI agent for cold outreach discovery + AI drafting. Structured where structure works, intelligent where it needs to adapt.

Pros

  • Best of both worlds
  • Reliable + flexible
  • Covers active + cold targets
  • Cost-efficient

Cons

  • More to build
  • Two discovery paths to maintain

Why C is recommended

Job boards catch companies actively hiring — highest conversion. The AI agent layer then discovers companies that aren't posting publicly (startups, research labs) for cold outreach. Best coverage, reasonable cost.
